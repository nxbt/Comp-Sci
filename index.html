What is HTML?

HTML, or Hyper Text Markup Language, is a text formatting language designed for writting webpages. Every single page on the internet is written in HTML, including this one! With HTML, you can easily format a page using just text on your keyboard! To make a webpage, all you need to do is enter HTML into a text document on your computer, and then open it up in your internet browser. A brower's job is to translate HTML into formated text and acess these types of pages on the internet. If you want other people to acess your webpage, you need to upload your text document to a server, and then people need to open the domain.

HTML Tags

HTML utilizes 'tags' which are used to format your webpage. tags are surrounded in greater than and less than signs. (these things: < >) after you open a tag, you add text after it, then close the tag by adding another tag after your text that has a slash right inside the greater than sign. Tags let you format your page in a variety of ways. for example, the 'p' tag creates a paragraph, that will display text and then carry over to a new line when you end it. The h1 - h6 tags let you create headers, which are bigger than normal text and are also bolded.

Some tags

  • p- creates a paragraph
  • b- creates bolded text
  • table- creates a table
  • tr- creates a row in a table
  • td- creates a box in a table row

Attributes

Attributes are pieces of text that go inside of the opening tag. Attributes allow you to change how the text inside the tag works. For example, the style tag allows you to change the color of the text to red by typing: style="color:red" or, you can assign a tag a class with: class="className" where className is the name of your class. There's not much use of class with just HTML though, to use the class attribute, you need to use CSS.

Some Attributes

  • style- changes the text style
  • class- gives the tag a class
  • id- gives the tag an id
  • title- displays a title above text

What is CSS?

CSS, which stands for Cascading Style Sheets, is used to give more control over your HTML websites. With CSS, you can change the color of text and backgrounds, the font size for certain tags, the type of font, and much more. If this is sounding like the style attribute, that's because the style attribute is actually inline CSS! What's better about writing CSS in its own document is that, for example, you can change all of the p tags to have red text and just change the text color once! You can also change the styling of all text that has a certain class, if you want more control. With CSS, you can quickly and easily change the style of any text on your webpage.

Computer Hardware

There are many components required for a computer to run. Every computer needs a motherboard, CPU, RAM, a hard drive, and a power supply. Without these components, a computer cannot run. Most computers also have a mouse, keyboard, and monitor, but servers and some other computers don't need them to be used.

The Motherboard

The motherboard connects all of the other components of the computer together. Every other component needs to be directly connected to the motherboard to be a part of the computer. It can be though of like a backbone, or a heart. The actually connections between the motherboard and other components are called buses. If the motherboard is the heart, buses are the veins and arteries, sending data to and from the motherboard.


The CPU

The CPU, or Central Processing Unit, does most of the calculations for the computer. In this way, it is the brains of the computer. CPU's are very small and attach directly to your motherboard. Because of the large amount of computing they do, CPU's generally have small fans and heat sinks right on them!

The RAM

The RAM, or Random Access Memory, is un-saveable memory that can be accessed on the fly by any program. It is meant to story data temporarily, but do so very quickly. Any changes to a document made before it is saved is stored here. This is because data can be stored more easily if there is power, but saving data when the power turns off is much harder and time consuming.

The Hard Drive

The hard drive is a disk inside of your computer that can be rewritten to permanently store data on your computer, even when the power turns off. Because data has to be physically written to the hard drive, it takes some time for data to be stored or read, which is why RAM is used for quicker storeage.


The Power Supply

The power supply is what gives power to the components of the computer. It takes in electricity, and rations it to all of the componets that need it. The more powerful the parts of your computer are, more electricity your power supply need to convert to keep your computer running smoothly.


Other Stuff

Other important parts of your computer are the mouse, keyboard, and monitor. The mouse is a handheld device, generally with two buttons on it. It can be moved around on a flat surface to move your cursor on the computer. It can be used to quickly influence graphical components in a simple way, and is required to use most programs. A keyboard is a pad of buttons that can be pushed to type out words or commands. Getting good with typing and learning keyboard shortcuts is required for quickly navigating computers. Finally, the monitor is a screen that quickly refreshes to display images or text to the user. Most monitors Are LCD, or Liquid Crystal Display, and block light in small amounts to change the colors of the screen. Without a monitor, it is next to impossible to know what you are doing on a computer.
